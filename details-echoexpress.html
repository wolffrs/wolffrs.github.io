Echo Express is an murder-mystery educational puzzle game designed for children aged 11 to 14. Set aboard a train, players take on the role of a detective tasked with solving a murder by conversating with characters and distinguishing their biases from the truth. The game aims to teach players about filter bubbles and cognitive biases in a fun and interactive way.

Gameplay Phases
Phase 1 (observation): Players must listen carefully to conversations taking place in each train carriage. It is up to the player to deduce which filter bubble corresponds to each carriage.
Phase 1 (bias recognition): In each filter bubble (carriage), players encounter and learn about two cognitive biases. They observe how these biases influence characters' perceptions and actions.
Phase 3 (conclusion): Players use the gathered information to solve the murder!

Echo Express is the final result of my graduation project, which has been well received for its strong connection to the target audience, its ability to make complex information easily understandable, its visual design and the enjoyement of learning through gameplay.
I've worked on this project individually for 18 weeks and graduated with a 9.5.
